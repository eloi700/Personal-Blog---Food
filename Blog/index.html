Vietnam

Vietnamese cuisine encompasses with different distinctive flavor. Their cooking has often been characterized with fresh ingredients, like raw vegetables as condiments for their dishes to combine properly with each main dish in flavor. The vegetables principally are herbs and wild edible vegetables gathered from forests and family gardens. Leaves and buds are the most common parts of vegetables used.

Thailand

Thai cooking places emphasis on lightly prepared dishes with strong aromatic components and a spicy edge. Traditional Thai cuisine loosely falls into four categories: tom (boiled dishes), yam (spicy salads), tam (pounded foods), and gaeng (curries). Thai meal would normally consist of rice with several dishes which should form a harmonious contrast of flavors and textures as well as preparation methods. And traditionally, a meal would have at least five elements. A dip or relish for raw or cooked vegetables (khrueang chim) is the most crucial component of any Thai meal.

Philippines

Filipino cuisine is from the food traditions of various ethno linguistic groups and tribes. During the pre-Hispanic era in the Philippines, the preferred Austronesian methods for food preparation were boiling, steaming, and roasting. Filipino cuisine centers on the combination of sweet, sour, and salty. Different regions have its different base of cooking flavor like in Bicol they are known for spicy. Their staple food is rice and often steamed, served with meat, fish and vegetable dishes. Leftover rice is often fried with garlic (sinangag), which is usually served at breakfast together with a fried egg and cured meat or sausages.

Malaysia

Malaysian cuisine is a mixture of various food cultures from around Malay Archipelago, India, China, the Middle East, and several European countries. Both rice and noodles are staple food in Malaysia. Malay cuisine bears many similarities to Indonesian cuisine, in particular some of the regional traditions from Sumatra. It has also been influenced by Chinese, Indian, Thai and many other cultures throughout history, producing a distinct cuisine of their own.

Indonesia

Indonesian cuisine has a wide variety of recipes, composed of different ethnic groups. Many regional cuisines exist, often based upon indigenous culture with some foreign influences. Indonesian spice (Rempah) and the mixture of spices / seasoning (bumbu) appears in the names of certain spice mixtures, sauces and seasoning pastes. The Indonesian fondness for hot and spicy food – the intensity ranges from mild to very hot. Mostly, they make it sambal and an example is sambal terasi. Sambal terasi is use to be the base ingredient for many dishes, they make.

Cambodia

Cambodian cuisine is an umbrella term for the cuisines of all ethnic groups in Cambodia. Like, Khmer cuisine - refers specifically to the cuisine of the Khmer people. Khmer cuisine can be classified into peasant, elite and royal cuisine. Meals in Cambodia are usually freshly prepared three times a day (for breakfast, lunch and dinner), although in rural areas only breakfast and dinner may be eaten. All dishes are served and eaten simultaneously. A number of side dishes are usually served alongside the main dishes. Moreover, a variety of condiments, such as chili jam, pickled green chilies, sugar, garlic flakes, fish sauce and soy sauce, are also available.
